What is CS Materials?

The CS Materials system has been developed as part of an NSF CyberTraining award, for generating and disseminating high quality, engaging learning materials to serve as a shared resource for CS educators. The system provides instructors with the ability to share, find, and adopt materials that align with national CS education standards, embody evidence-based approaches for increased student engagement and improved student outcomes, while maintaining course rigor. The proposed system is developed to support mappings to multiple standards and understand their relationships; currently it supports the ACM 2013 CS curriculum guidelines (the latest that is available) and the IEEE/ACM TCPP guidelines for Parallel and Distributed Computing. See the website home page for more examples and a webinar video on an introduction to CS Materials and its capabilities and benefits for computing related educators.

What does CS Materials mean to Instructors, i.e. You, and Your Students?

The need to have high quality student centered courses that incorporate learning outcomes, use relevant pedagogies and engaging materials have put increasing amounts of pressure on instructors who are teaching courses with high enrollments, especially in early CS courses. CS Materials provides a system that permits courses to be classified to the latest curriculum standards, and allows instructors to ensure alignment between different components of their course, for instance, if their lectures are aligned to exams. They also have the means to search for specific materials (lecture, or an assignment, or a video) that meet their learning outcomes, compare their course content against similar courses at other institutions, or within their own institution (multiple sections of the same course, course sequence inconsistencies or gaps, etc.). Insights generated from analyzing a course in CS Materials could lead to revisions, resulting in improvements that will benefit students.

Call for Participation

We are interested in partners who are willing to input their courses into CS Materials and benefit from its various course design features described above, as well use it to find relevant and engaging learning materials. The project team will provide full support and if needed, meetings with the instructor to ease the learning curve. Instructor stipends are available to support participants. After a course is input into CS Materials, the project team will help interpret and analyze the participant's course content, including comparison to other similar courses in CS Materials.

How much of a Time Commitment does Inputting a Course Involve?

Typically a course requires 1 to 1.5 day of work. It depends to a large extent on the number of materials that make up the course. Each material can be a lecture slide deck, video, assignment, project, reading materials, exam. It is best to enter them as individual materials as this makes the analysis more meaningful. The initial materials typically take the longest time, but once the classification guidelines become more familiar to the instructor, the process can get a lot faster.
